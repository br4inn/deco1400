AW11

The Toyota MR2 AW11, often fondly referred to as the "AW11," stands as an iconic representative of Toyota's engineering prowess and commitment to delivering an exhilarating driving experience. Introduced in 1984, the AW11 was the first generation of the MR2 series, setting the benchmark for compact, mid-engine sports cars. At the heart of the AW11 lies its mid-engine layout, which enhances its balance and agility, making it a joy to drive on winding roads and tight corners. This configuration, combined with its lightweight construction, offers remarkable handling characteristics that have delighted driving enthusiasts for decades. The AW11 was available with a range of engines, including the rev-happy 1.6-liter 4A-GE inline-four engine, which became legendary for its performance and reliability. Paired with either a manual or automatic transmission, the AW11 delivered an engaging driving experience, with precise shifting and responsive throttle. Despite its compact dimensions, the AW11 boasted a well-designed interior that provided comfort and convenience for its occupants. Its futuristic design cues, including pop-up headlights and sleek body lines, added to its appeal and contributed to its timeless aesthetic. Over the years, the MR2 AW11 has garnered a dedicated following among automotive enthusiasts worldwide, thanks to its blend of performance, handling, and reliability. Whether carving through mountain roads or navigating city streets, the AW11 continues to captivate drivers with its spirited performance and timeless charm.

Explore Changes

1986 (MY 1987)

  • Expanded cosmetic options:
    • Color-matched bumpers
    • Side stripes
    • Mudflaps
    • Side skirts
  • Leather interior option
  • Engine upgrades:
    • Larger flywheel/clutch for naturally aspirated models (Japan)
  • Safety updates:
    • Third brake light

1988 (MY 1989)

  • Supercharged model introduced (Japan only)
  • T-bar roof option available
  • New exterior colors offered
  • Updated interior trim options
  • Improved sound insulation for quieter cabin

1989 (MY 1990)

  • T-bar roof: Mirrored (opaque) glass panels instead of smoked (clear)
  • Exterior changes:
    • Color-matched door handles and side mirrors for all models
    • LED third brake light integrated into the rear spoiler
    • More aerodynamic wing mirrors, optional with power retraction
  • North American supercharged models:
    • Rear anti-roll bar added
