The first MR2, launched in 1984, was a lightweight and well-handling 2-seater sports car. It was known for its mid-engine layout and nimble performance, thanks in part to help from Lotus engineers during development. The most common version came with a 1.6-liter engine and a 5-speed manual transmission, offering a 0-60 mph time in the 8-second range. In 1986, a supercharged version was introduced, boosting horsepower to 145 hp and accelerating from 0-100 km/h in under 7 seconds. This model was exclusive to Japan and North America.

1986 (MY 1987): Supercharged model introduced (Japan only). T-bar roof option available. The 1986 facelift (MK1b) for the MR2 brought several improvements: Engine: Stronger engine internals for the naturally aspirated version, with a slight power bump in North America (115 hp). Brakes: Larger front and rear discs for better stopping power. Suspension: Revised rear suspension for improved handling.Exterior: Updated bumpers, taillights (except UK), front lip, and antenna placement. T-bar roof option added.Interior: New center console, armrest, glovebox lock, updated radio and speakers, revised door panels, steering wheel, and gauges. Additional changes for left-hand drive models.

1988 (MY 1989) changes:T-bar roof: Mirrored (opaque) glass panels instead of smoked (clear). Exterior: Color-matched door handles and side mirrors for all models. LED third brake light integrated into the rear spoiler. More aerodynamic wing mirrors, optional with power retraction.North American supercharged models: Rear anti-roll bar added.
